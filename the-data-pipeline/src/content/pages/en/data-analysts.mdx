Data Analysts:

* Discuss with end users looking for insights to understand their needs and translate them into a rigorous analytical process.
* Draw data from the gold layer and apply the necessary analysis to it.
* Communicate the insights they obtain, usually via dashboards or ad-hoc reports and presentations.

With Data Scientists, they are responsible for one of the last "conduits" of the Data Pipeline – the communication of analysis results to the organization's decision-makers. Most commonly, they bring value by reducing uncertainty around decision-making.

Their work is often driven by immediate business needs but leads to both one-off ad-hoc analyses and recurring reporting or dashboards. In a Data team, they are generally among the most deeply familiar with the domain and stakeholders they support within the organization.

### Examples of questions a Data Analyst can answer:

- What was the YoY progression of the number of active users on our app last month?
- Does this change in our product (e.g., a new feature) increase user satisfaction?
- Why is our [B2B](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Business-to-business) revenue growth plateauing?
