### Data Ingestion

Organizations wish to leverage any data relevant to their activities and decision-making. Therefore, the flow of data Data Professionals deal with originates from various sources. Depending on the organization, the source systems can be:

- The software the organization owns, develops, and distributes to clients whose actions on the software may be tracked (e.g., YouTube for Google);
- Software used by the organization, internally, to conduct its operations such as a customer relationship management system (e.g., Pipedrive), where the interactions between a company and its customers are logged, or an emailing platform (e.g., Mailjet) which automates the sending of emails;
- Third-party source systems providing information relevant to the organization such as exchange rates for organizations trading in multiple currencies, for instance.

The collection of data from source systems is the realm of Data Engineers. They develop the software necessary to collect this data regularly (i.e., in a timely and accurate manner) – this is the first "conduit" of the Data Pipeline.

Each source system differs from the others in a dimension or another. Those dimensions Data Engineers have to navigate are:

- The authentication necessary to gain access to the source system and its data,
- The format of the data,
- The speed and frequency with which it can be collected,
- The way the data can be read: for example, is it possible to read only some records of interest, or is it only possible to download the entire dataset (which may be large)?
- The fact that the data's structure can change over time whereas downstream operators need a certain stability,
- Etc.

Once the raw data is extracted from source systems, Data Engineers load it as tables into a database centralizing all the data the organization wants to extract value from. Several designs of central databases exist, each with its specificities, but a common variant is the [**Data Warehouse**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Data_warehouse). This is the "storage facility" of the Data Pipeline.

### Infrastructure

Data Engineers are also responsible for the setup and maintenance of the infrastructure the Data team relies upon: the Data Warehouse used by all team members, and [business intelligence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Business_intelligence) tools used by Data Analysts to make dashboards, for instance.

They are also the ones in charge of deploying the prediction models developed by Data Scientists (who will be introduced in the last section).
